About Act17:11

"Now the Berean Jews were of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day to see if what Paul said was true."

— Acts 17:11 (NIV)

The Problem

YouTube has become one of the largest platforms for biblical, historical, and theological content. Creators can reach millions of viewers with claims about Scripture, church history, and doctrine — but there's no built-in way for audiences to know if what they're hearing is accurate.

X (Twitter) has Community Notes. Wikipedia has citations. YouTube has nothing.

A creator can misquote Scripture, reverse historical chronology, or caricature entire theological traditions — and the average viewer has no way to know. Act17:11 exists to change that.

What We Do

We analyze YouTube videos that make factual claims about the Bible, church history, theology, and related topics. For each video, we:

1

Extract every factual claim from the transcript — dates, quotes, attributions, historical assertions, scriptural references.

2

Check each claim against scholarly sources, primary texts, and historical records. Every claim is rated: true, false, misleading, or disputed.

3

Detect rhetorical stratagems — logical fallacies, cherry-picking, decontextualization, and other persuasion techniques identified using Schopenhauer's 38 stratagems framework.

4

Generate a Truth Score — a cumulative accuracy rating that tracks each creator over time, so audiences can calibrate how much to trust what they hear.

How Scoring Works

80%+
Reliable
Mostly accurate. Minor errors on peripheral details.
60-79%
Caution
Mix of accurate and inaccurate claims. Verify before citing.
<60%
Unreliable
Significant factual errors. Core claims likely false.

Weighted scoring: Not all errors are equal. We weight claims by importance:

CORE · 3x Thesis-level claims that carry the argument
SUPPORT · 2x Evidence claims that back the thesis
BACKGROUND · 1x Incidental details like dates and numbers

Steelman principle: Every analysis includes a steelman — the strongest possible version of the speaker's argument. We're not here to tear people down. We're here to help audiences think critically.

What We're Not

✕

Not a hit job site. We include true claims, scholarly splits, and steelman arguments. Creators who are accurate score well.

✕

Not denominational. We check facts against primary sources and scholarly consensus, not against any particular theological tradition.

✕

Not opinion-based. We don't score theological interpretations. We score verifiable factual claims — did they cite the right book? Did the event happen when they said it did?

Why "Act17:11"?

In Acts 17:11, the Berean Jews are praised for not simply accepting what they were told — they went back to the source texts and checked for themselves. They didn't reject the message. They didn't blindly accept it. They verified it.

That's what this site does. We give you the tools to be a Berean in the age of YouTube.
